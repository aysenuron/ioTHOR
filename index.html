Protect Your Temperature Sensitive Inventory

TempTHOR measures the temperature of your temperature sensetive inventory continuously, sends immediate notifications if needed, and protects your inventory.

Learn More

What Does TempTHOR Device Do?

Makes Your Cooling Units Smart

TempTHOR makes your refrigerators, freezers, and other cooling units smart. It measures temperature continuously and consistently, day and night.

Sends Notifications

TempTHOR alerts you in case the temperature exceeds any user defined threshold, it sends an immediate notification via email and text.

Power Outage Protection

In case of a power outage, TempTHOR continues to keep and store measurements.

On the Cloud

Cloud-based infrastructure makes it super-easy to setup & manage your TempTHOR devices.

Why Do You Need to Protect Your Inventory?

You have delicate and temperature sensitive products that need to be protected 24/7.

You should be aware of the temperature of the cooling unit to avoid spoilage.

You need to take immediate action if there is a power outage or malfunction in the cooling unit.

Regulations & Best Practices

CDC provides best practices for vaccine storage and handling from Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) recommendations, product information from vaccine manufacturers, and scientific studies.

FDA Quality System Regulation Section 820 states that “Each manufacturer shall establish and maintain procedures for the control of storage areas and stock rooms for product to prevent mix-ups, damage, deterioration, contamination, or other adverse effects pending use or distribution and to ensure that no obsolete, rejected, or deteriorated product is used or distributed.”

Keeping foods chilled at proper temperatures is one of the best ways to prevent or slow the growth of Salmonella or E.Coli bacteria. -Keep your appliances at the proper temperatures. -Keep the refrigerator temperature at or below 40° F (4° C). -The freezer temperature should be 0° F (-18° C). -Check temperatures periodically. -Appliance thermometers are the best way of knowing these temperatures.
